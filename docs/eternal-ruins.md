# WANDERING ALONE

There are wanderers who travel with others, and wanderers who do not. The ruins do not distinguish between them.

When you walk alone, the ruins speak more clearly. You move at your own pace, take your own risks, and write your own record of what you find.

This chapter contains what you need to explore the Eternal Ruins by yourself. The core rules apply throughout. The dice read the same, the tracks fill and clear the same, and your wanderer is built exactly as described in the Character Elements chapter. What changes is everything the Guide would normally decide.

## WHAT YOU NEED

Everything from the Basic Rules chapter, plus:

A **Solo Sheet**, which combines your Character Sheet with a few additions: the Omen Track, an expanded Food Track, and space for Clues.

Six **d6s**.

A **journal**. A notebook, loose pages, a tablet. Format does not matter.

## THE ORACLE

When you play alone, you will sometimes need to ask whether something is true. Whether a spirit is willing to speak. Whether a door is locked from this side or the other. Whether something has been here before you.

The Oracle uses the same result spread as every other roll in the game.

### BUILDING THE POOL

Before rolling, consider how likely the thing you are asking about actually is, based on what you know of the current situation.

- **Unlikely** (the answer would surprise you): roll 1d6
- **Possible** (it could go either way): roll 2d6
- **Likely** (everything points toward yes): roll 3d6

Roll all dice and take the highest result.

| Result     | Meaning                   |
| ---------- | ------------------------- |
| 6          | Yes, and...               |
| 4 or 5     | Yes, but... or No, but... |
| 1, 2, or 3 | No, and...                |

A result of 4 or 5 is a choice: yes or no, but either way something complicates the outcome. You decide which fits better.

**Doubles** on an Oracle roll produce a **Twist**: something outside the scope of the question itself. Roll the Spark Tables if you need direction.

### ASKING THE RIGHT QUESTIONS

The Oracle works best with specific, binary questions. "Will the demon notice me?" gets a cleaner answer than "What does the demon do?" For open questions about what a place suggests or what an NPC wants, use the Spark Tables.

## EXPLORATION ALONE

Exploration scenes work the same as in group play with one difference: there is no party to fill the travel positions. A lone wanderer manages pace, watchfulness, foraging, and charting at once, and cannot do all of them well.

### THE JOURNEY ROLL

When you move from one location to the next, make a single **Journey Roll**. Before rolling, choose a **Focus**: what your wanderer is paying most attention to during this leg of travel.

Build your dice pool as you would any action roll: relevant edge, skill ranks, any helpful aspects or relics, and one advantage die if conditions allow. The Region Track advances when you arrive at each new location.

**WARY**
_You move carefully, watching the way ahead._

Pace: 2 ticks | Relevant skills: Hunt, Muse

| Result     | Outcome                                                                                             |
| ---------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6          | Roll twice for the next location's Feature and choose which to use. Roll the Detail once as normal. |
| 4 or 5     | Arrive without incident.                                                                            |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Arrive, and mark the Omen Track once.                                                               |

**SWIFT**
_You move quickly, covering ground at the cost of attention._

Pace: 1 tick | Relevant skills: Scramble, Delve

| Result     | Outcome                                                                                      |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6          | Arrive and take one material oddment from the oddment list of the current Feature or Detail. |
| 4 or 5     | Arrive. Roll the encounter die as normal.                                                    |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Arrive. Roll the encounter die and mark the Omen Track once.                                 |

**FORAGE**
_You move at a relaxed pace, gathering what the ruins offer._

Pace: 2 ticks | Relevant skills: Hunt, Delve, Scramble

| Result     | Outcome                                                                          |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6          | Take two edible oddments from the oddment list of the current Feature or Detail. |
| 4 or 5     | Take one edible oddment.                                                         |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Find nothing and mark the Omen Track once.                                       |

**CHART**
_You move methodically, noting what surrounds you._

Pace: 2 ticks | Relevant skills: Puzzle

| Result     | Outcome                                                                                                                                                      |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 6          | Gain a fragment. If you have two or more fragments, you may combine any two to form a Discovery. Roll a third fragment randomly to represent the unexpected. |
| 4 or 5     | Gain a fragment.                                                                                                                                             |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Arrive with nothing noted and mark the Omen Track once.                                                                                                      |

If the Omen Track was never marked across an entire bell of travel, gain a fragment of your choice.

## OMENS AND ENCOUNTERS

Encounters do not arrive without warning. The Omen Track gives you notice first.

### THE OMEN TRACK

Your Solo Sheet has a three-box track marked Omens. It resets at the start of each bell.

When you mark the Omen Track, roll a d6 and record the omen in your journal:

| Roll | Omen                                                                            |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | Territorial signs: scratches on the stone, a carcass stripped clean nearby      |
| 2    | Something heard in the distance, gone when you stop to listen                   |
| 3    | Traces: footprints, a smear on the wall, an oddment disturbed from where it lay |
| 4    | Burrowballs gone completely still, birds rising suddenly                        |
| 5    | Sprites gesturing urgently, pointing away from something                        |
| 6    | A shadow at the edge of sight                                                   |

**When all three Omen Track boxes are marked**, an encounter triggers at the start of the next location. Roll d6: 1-3 is a dangerous encounter, 4-5 is neutral, 6 is beneficial. Use the Spark Tables to give it detail.

### INVESTIGATING AN OMEN

To investigate a sign you have found, spend a tick and roll Hunt or Muse:

| Result     | Outcome                                                                   |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6          | Clear one Omen Track mark and learn the encounter type before it arrives. |
| 4 or 5     | Learn the encounter type in advance.                                      |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Mark the Omen Track again.                                                |

### RETREATING

When retreating from a hazard, roll the most relevant Moving skill:

| Result     | Outcome                                                      |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 6          | Clean escape.                                                |
| 4 or 5     | Escape, but mark the Omen Track once.                        |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Escape, take 1 mark of damage, and mark the Omen Track once. |

## HAZARDS ALONE

### TRACK REDUCTION

Reduce all **Challenge**, **Accord**, and **Override** tracks by 2 boxes (minimum 2 boxes). Countdown tracks are not reduced.

Named, unique hazards such as the listed demons are reduced by 1 box only.

### DESPERATE EFFORT

Once per encounter, spend a point of Hope to mark 2 boxes on any track on a Triumph, rather than the usual 1. On a Critical Triumph, mark 3 boxes.

## THE SOLITARY CAMP

### FOOD

The Food Track on your Solo Sheet has **three boxes** rather than two. Morale rolls trigger only when two or more boxes are unfilled when the ticking clock completes.

**Minimum Ingredients**: You need only **2 edible oddments** to prepare a basic meal, rather than the usual 3. A basic meal still marks two boxes on the Food Track. Fewer than 2 oddments produces a substandard meal, which marks one box.

**Opportunistic Foraging**: Once per location, before or after any other action, you may roll a single d6 to look for something edible:

| Result     | Outcome                                                                       |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5 or 6     | Take 1 edible oddment from the oddment list of the current Feature or Detail. |
| 4          | Take 1 edible oddment with a negative tag such as Stale or Unappetising.      |
| 1, 2, or 3 | Find nothing.                                                                 |

**Banking Flavour Points**: If a meal generates more Flavour Points than you can spend, you may carry up to 2 of them into your next meal. They are spent first.

### TASKS

You take one task at camp, as normal. Music and Storytelling clears marks from your own habit aspects or Curse gauge, as there is no one else to perform for.

### CAMPING ENCOUNTERS

Roll for camping encounters as normal. The Oracle and Spark Tables handle what arrives and how it behaves.

## THE MYSTERY

The ruins are full of stories without endings. A chamber collapsed with no obvious cause. A demon sanctum that smells of something gentler underneath the filth. A wall carved with the same face sixty times, each one slightly different, stopping abruptly at a crack.

The Mystery system lets you piece these stories together and decide what they mean.

### CLUES

A **Clue** is a fragment of evidence: something seen, found, or felt that points toward a larger story. Write each Clue as a short phrase in your journal.

_"Burn marks that face inward." "A name carved only once, in a different hand." "A child's bedroll in the corner of the demon's sanctum."_

You gather Clues by:

- Arriving at a **Library**, **Relic Vault**, **Demon Sanctum**, or any location with the **Ominous** or **Collapsed** detail: gain one Clue automatically
- Rolling a 6 on any investigation-focused action using Puzzle, Muse, Hunt, or similar
- Asking the Oracle whether there is more to a place: a yes result includes a Clue
- Receiving information from a spirit, sprite, or NPC that does not quite add up

Each Clue belongs to a **Mystery**: a question about what happened somewhere, to someone. You do not declare Mysteries. They emerge as Clues connect.

### MAKING A DEDUCTION

When you have **three or more Clues** connected to the same Mystery, you may attempt a **Deduction Roll**. Build a dice pool using Puzzle or Muse, plus any relevant edges, aspects, or relics.

| Result     | Outcome                                                                                                               |
| ---------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 6          | Your theory holds. Write it in your journal as fact. Gain a fragment and clear one mark from your Curse gauge.        |
| 4 or 5     | Mostly correct, but one element stays uncertain. Write your theory with a single question remaining. Gain a fragment. |
| 1, 2, or 3 | You have misread something. Remove one Clue from this Mystery and try again when you find another.                    |

**Critical on a Triumph**: Your theory is correct and opens a new thread. A new Mystery begins.
**Critical on a Disaster**: Your wrong theory leads you somewhere dangerous. Mark the Omen Track.

Whatever you write as your theory is the truth of this place. There is no hidden answer.

## THE JOURNAL

**After arriving at a location**: Two or three sentences. What you see. What strikes you about it.

**After a Disaster on a meaningful roll**: Write the consequence without softening it.

**After a Triumph on a meaningful roll**: Write what it felt like.

**After making camp**: A longer entry. What the fire looked like. What you cooked.

**After a Deduction Roll**: Write your theory in full, regardless of the result.

**When a Fallout or Curse gauge increases**: Write the condition and name it.

**When Hope shifts**: Note what changed it.

**When you sketch**: Draw the location, the creature, the relic found. The quality of the drawing is not the point.

Some wanderers write in the first person, addressing a future reader. Some write like a naturalist cataloguing findings. Some write to someone absent.

## GUIDE FUNCTIONS

**NPC reactions**: Oracle. Set likelihood based on what you know of the current situation.

**What an NPC wants**: Spark Tables. Combine Theme and Action.

**Encounter flavour**: Omen table plus Spark Tables.

**Consequences of ambiguous rolls**: Spark Tables, read as a complication.

**Hidden track lengths**: Roll a single d6 before starting the encounter. That is the track length.

**Demon aura effects**: Apply the one most fitting to the current location.

**Spirit offerings**: Oracle. The quality of the offering sets the likelihood.

## DEATH AND WHAT COMES AFTER

When a wanderer reaches their Final Moments alone, write the last entry.

A newly-awakened wanderer who comes after might find evidence of the one who came before: a journal left behind, a relic without an owner, a name carved into the stone.

## QUICK REFERENCE

**Oracle**: Unlikely 1d6, Possible 2d6, Likely 3d6. Take highest. 6 = Yes and, 4-5 = Yes but / No but, 1-3 = No and. Doubles = Twist.

**Journey Roll**: Choose Focus (Wary / Swift / Forage / Chart), build pool, roll. Read result per Focus table.

**Omen Track**: 3 boxes, resets each bell. When full, encounter triggers at next location. Investigate by spending a tick and rolling Hunt or Muse.

**Hazard Tracks**: Challenge, Accord, Override reduced by 2 (min 2). Countdowns unchanged. Named hazards reduced by 1.

**Desperate Effort**: Once per encounter, spend Hope to mark 2 boxes on Triumph. Critical Triumph marks 3.

**Food Track**: 3 boxes solo. Minimum 2 oddments for a basic meal. Opportunistic Forage: 1d6 once per location, no task required. 5-6 = 1 edible oddment, 4 = oddment with negative tag, 1-3 = nothing.

**Mystery**: Gather 3+ Clues. Deduction Roll using Puzzle or Muse. 6 = theory is fact, gain fragment, clear Curse mark. 4-5 = mostly correct, gain fragment. 1-3 = wrong, remove one Clue.

**Journal**: Write after arrivals, Triumphs, Disasters, camp, deductions, gauge increases, Hope shifts.
